# Reflection

**What should be public, what should stay hidden, and what should be decided by AI versus by a human in a bounty system?**

The rules of the game should be fully public: the rubric, the reward, the deadlines, who committed, when the judging happened, and the final verdict with its reasoning — because participants can only trust a competition they can audit. The content of submissions should stay hidden for exactly as long as it gives anyone an unfair edge: during the submission phase in commit-reveal, and ideally through the judging step itself in a TEE-based design, after which everything should be revealed so losers can verify they were judged on what they actually wrote. Salts, encryption keys, and any storage credentials should never be public at all. The AI is the right tool for the comparative work humans do badly at scale: reading all submissions together, scoring them against the rubric consistently, and explaining its ranking. But the AI's output is a recommendation, not a payment instruction — model outputs can be malformed, manipulated by prompt injection hidden inside a submission, or simply wrong. So the human owner should hold the final, accountable decision to release funds, and the contract should enforce that even that human can only pay a validly revealed participant. Public rules, temporarily hidden answers, AI for judgment at scale, humans for accountability — that split is what makes the bounty both fair and safe.
